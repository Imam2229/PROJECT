Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about blood donation

Most people in good health, at least 17 years old (16 with parental consent in some areas), and weighing at least 110 pounds can donate blood. There are some medical conditions and medications that may require a waiting period or make you ineligible.

You can donate whole blood every 56 days (8 weeks). Platelet donations can be made more frequently - up to 24 times per year. Your body replaces the donated blood components within a few weeks.

Yes, donating blood is very safe. New, sterile equipment is used for each donor, so there's no risk of contracting a bloodborne infection. Most people feel fine after donating, though some might feel lightheaded. The entire process takes about an hour from start to finish.

  • Get a good night's sleep before donating
  • Eat a healthy meal and drink plenty of fluids
  • Avoid fatty foods before donation
  • Bring your ID and list of medications you're taking
  • Wear clothing with sleeves that can be rolled up
